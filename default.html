APPETIZERS↑

An appetizer (also known as an hors d'oeuvre) is a small dish served before a meal. An appetizer is meant to stimulate your appetite, making you extra hungry for your meal. appetizers are intended to be eaten by hand in a bite or two, or at least be capable of being cut with the side of a fork, rather than a knife.

MAIN DISHES↑

The main dish is usually the heaviest, heartiest, and most complex or substantial dish in a meal. It is most often preceded by an appetizer, soup or salad, and followed by a dessert

The main ingredient is usually meat or fish; in vegetarian meals, the main dish sometimes attempts to mimic a meat course. In formal dining, a well-planned main dish can function as a sort of gastronomic apex or climax. In such a scheme, the preceding dishes are designed to prepare for and lead up to the main dish in such a way that the main dish is anticipated

DESSERTS↑

Dessert is a course that concludes a meal. The course usually consists of sweet foods, such as confections, and possibly a beverage such as dessert wine or liqueur.

The importance of dessert on the menu is to make us feel satisfied after a meal and compensates for low blood sugar. it desires to improve your mood by ingesting sugar. They increase the production of the so-called Hormone Happiness
